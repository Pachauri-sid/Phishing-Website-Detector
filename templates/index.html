What is phishing?

        Phishing is a type of online scam where a malicious actor, also known as a "phisher, " attempts to trick individuals into sharing their sensitive information, such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal information. Phishing attacks can occur through various methods, such as emails, text messages, social media messages, or even phone calls.

        Phishers typically pose as trustworthy entities, such as banks, government agencies, or popular websites, and create fake messages or websites that look legitimate in order to deceive their victims. The messages or websites often contain urgent or threatening language to pressure the victims into taking action without thinking critically. For example, a phishing email may claim that there is an issue with a user's bank account and request that they click on a link to resolve the issue, which would then lead them to a fake website designed to steal their login credentials.



Features Used for Website Phishing Detection

        There are many algorithms and various types of phishing that look for types of information in educational materials and products. Phishing websites and related pages have several characteristics that distinguish them from malicious URLs. For example; Attackers can register long and confusing names to hide the real name (squatting, phishing). Sometimes an attacker can use a direct IP address instead of a domain name. Such an event is beyond our scope, but may be used for the same purpose. In addition to URL-based features, various features used in machine learning algorithms are used in the research process. How to protect mobiles and laptops from phishing attack?

        Phishing attacks are one of the most common forms of cyber-attacks, and they can be quite dangerous as they can lead to the theft of sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and financial information. Here are some steps you can take to protect your mobiles and laptops from phishing attacks:

1- Install Antivirus Software:- Ensure that your mobile and laptop have antivirus software installed, and keep it up-to-date to protect against any known threats.

2- Keep Software Updated:- Keep your operating system, web browsers, and other software up-to-date with the latest security patches, as these can help protect against known vulnerabilities.

3- Be Wary of Suspicious Emails:- Be cautious of emails that ask for sensitive information or that appear to be from a legitimate source, but contain suspicious links or attachments. Avoid clicking on any links or downloading any attachments from suspicious emails.

4- Verify Links Before Clicking:- Always verify the authenticity of links before clicking on them. Hover over the link to see the URL, and check that it is legitimate and matches the domain name of the sender.

5- Use Two-Factor Authentication:- Enable two-factor authentication on all of your accounts, which adds an extra layer of security to protect against unauthorized access.

6- Use Strong Passwords:- Use strong, unique passwords for each account, and avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

7- Use Virtual Private Networks (VPNs):- Use a VPN when accessing public Wi-Fi networks to encrypt your internet traffic and protect against eavesdropping.

By following these steps, you can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim to phishing attacks on your mobiles and laptops.
